    Escalation Priority Score (EPS) Methodology

    $$EPS = (30\% \cdot \text{Age}_{norm}) + (25\% \cdot \text{Reopen}_{norm}) + (25\% \cdot \text{Votes}_{norm}) + (20\% \cdot \text{Severity})$$

    Metric Components

    • Escalation Age (30%) Time elapsed since creation, normalized by ward-specific max age. Capped at 30 points.
    • Reopen Freq (25%) Frequency of grievance re-openings. Score maxes at 3+ reopens (systemic issue).
    • Net Votes (25%) Citizen sentiment (Upvotes - Downvotes), normalized by ward-specific max net-votes.
    • Severity (20%) Categorical baseline based on priority: High (20pts), Medium (12pts), Low (6pts).

    Priority Thresholds

    • Critical ≥ 75 — Immediate administrative intervention required.
    • High ≥ 50 — Priority response zone.
    • Moderate ≥ 25 — Standard escalation queue.
    • Low < 25 — Routine monitoring.

    Composite Calculation

    $$EPS = (30\% \cdot \text{Age}_{norm}) + (25\% \cdot \text{Reopen}_{norm}) + (25\% \cdot \text{Votes}_{norm}) + (20\% \cdot \text{Severity})$$

    Note on Normalization: Scores are calculated relative to the max values within each ward to ensure fair prioritization.

    In-depth Metrics

    Department Inflow ($T$) Resolutions ($R$) Caseload ($P$) Compliance ($S$) Recurrence ($\sum Ri$) Escalations ($E$) Efficiency Velocity DPI Performance Status

    Analytical Methodology & Performance Framework

    Metric Dimensions

    • $T$ (Inflow) Total volume of grievances received during the reporting period.
    • $R$ (Resolutions) Gross volume of grievances successfully processed and closed.
    • $P$ (Caseload) Current active workload consisting of pending and assigned tasks.
    • $S$ (Compliance) Volume of resolutions ($R$) successfully completed within the 48-hour SLA.
    • $\sum Ri$ (Recurrence) Aggregate intensity of grievances requiring redundant intervention.
    • $E$ (Escalations) Volume requiring administrative intervention at higher hierarchical levels.

    Performance Coefficients

    • Resolution Efficiency $\frac{R}{T}$: Quantifies gross output against total organizational demand.
    • Service Velocity $\frac{S}{R}$: Quantifies the timeliness of completed work (Adherence Rate).
    • Caseload Health $1 - \frac{P}{T}$: Evaluates the stability of the current department backlog.
    • Resolution Integrity $1 - \frac{\sum Ri}{R}$: Evaluates resolution accuracy and quality control.
    • Escalation Mitigation $1 - \frac{E}{T}$: Evaluates the department's ability to resolve issues locally.

    Department Performance Index (DPI)

    $DPI = (30\% \cdot \text{Res}) + (25\% \cdot \text{SLA}) + (20\% \cdot \text{Caseload}) + (15\% \cdot \text{Quality}) + (10\% \cdot \text{Escalation})$

    Administrative Note: Departments with zero grievance inflow ($T=0$) are assigned a baseline performance index of 70.0 to reflect organizational stability during inactive periods.

    Worker Analytics Methodology

    Worker Metrics

    • Resolved Number of grievance assignments completed by the worker within the selected period (7, 30, or 90 days).
    • SLA % $\frac{S}{R} \times 100$ where $S$ = assignments completed within 48-hour SLA, $R$ = total completed. Measures timeliness.
    • Rating Average citizen rating from grievance resolution feedback (GrievanceResolutionRating) in the period.
    • Attendance $\frac{\text{days with clock-in}}{\text{period days}} \times 100$ — proportion of working days the worker recorded attendance.

    Period Scope

    • Resolution Window Assignments completed between the period start and end dates are counted. Pending or in-progress tasks are excluded.
    • SLA Rule Completed within 48 hours of assignment: $\text{completed\_at} - \text{assigned\_at} \leq 48\,\text{h}$.
    • Status Current duty status from worker profile (e.g., onDuty, offDuty).

    Ward Analytics Methodology

    Ward Metrics

    • Total All grievances in the ward (includes pending, resolved, escalated).
    • Resolved Grievances closed with status resolved.
    • Pending Grievances not yet resolved (pending, assigned, in progress).
    • SLA Resolutions completed within 48 hours of creation: $\text{updated\_at} - \text{created\_at} \leq 48\,\text{h}$.
    • Escalated Grievances escalated to higher administrative levels.
    • Resolution % $\frac{R}{T} \times 100$ — proportion of total grievances that have been resolved.

    Ward Performance Index (WPI)

    $WPI = \frac{\sum \text{Department DPI}}{\text{Number of Departments active in ward}}$

    Each department's DPI is computed per (ward, department) using the same framework as the Departments tab. WPI is the average of those department scores for the ward. Wards with no department activity default to 70.0.

    Zone Analytics Methodology

    Zone Metrics

    • Total All grievances across all wards in the zone.
    • Resolved Grievances closed with status resolved.
    • Pending Grievances not yet resolved.
    • SLA Resolutions completed within 48 hours of creation.
    • Resolution % $\frac{R}{T} \times 100$ — proportion of total grievances resolved in the zone.

    Zone Performance Index (ZPI)

    $ZPI = \frac{\sum \text{Ward WPI}}{\text{Number of Wards in zone}}$

    ZPI is the average of Ward Performance Index (WPI) for all wards in the zone. Each ward's WPI is derived from department-level DPIs as defined in the Ward analytics methodology. Zones with no ward activity default to 70.0.

    Civic Impact Score (CIS)

    CIS (0–100) measures verified citizen participation: grievances filed, votes cast, comments, ward-level activity, and quality (e.g. non-spam) signals. Values use the latest stored snapshot per citizen (period dates are Indian Standard Time), from a rolling window of up to 7 days since the last global CIS run — same source as the Citizens list in Admin. Use Update CIS on the Citizens page to run now; the next automatic run is scheduled one week later (IST).

    Sustainability Methodology

    Sustainability Index (SI)

    $SI_{SDG} = \frac{\sum \text{DPI of mapped departments}}{\text{No. of mapped departments}}$

    For each SDG, departments are mapped from the comma-separated SDG field. Sustainability Index is the average DPI of mapped departments for that SDG. Add or edit SDG/description in Departments to keep this analysis accurate.
